# Bonus — Excel Pivot Automation Engine

> Turn a raw multi-channel campaign CSV into a formatted, multi-sheet Excel report in seconds. **Python + pandas + openpyxl.**

**Bonus / range piece** — a small Python tooling project, not one of the portfolio's core analyst headliners (see the [main README](../../README.md)). **Self-directed; the demo data is synthetic and labeled as such.**

## Problem

Analysts and marketers are routinely handed raw campaign exports — one row per channel × segment × day — and asked for the same summaries every time: spend and ROAS by channel, conversion rate by segment, the monthly trend, the top and bottom performers. Building those by hand in Excel is repetitive, slow, and easy to get subtly wrong — especially when different people define "ROAS" or "conversion rate" differently.

## Data

A **synthetic**, seeded dataset of ~1,600 rows: 6 months (Jan–Jun 2025), 4 channels (Email, Paid Search, Social, Display), 4 customer segments (New, Returning, VIP, Dormant).

| column | meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `date` | campaign day |
| `channel` | marketing channel |
| `segment` | customer segment |
| `spend` | ad spend ($) |
| `impressions` | times an ad was shown |
| `clicks` | clicks received |
| `conversions` | completed conversions |
| `revenue` | revenue generated ($) |

Generated by [`generate_demo_data.py`](generate_demo_data.py) and documented in [`data/README.md`](data/README.md). It is **not real** — it's shaped to be realistic so there's something genuine to analyze.

## Method

[`pivot_engine.py`](pivot_engine.py) reads the CSV, computes the summaries with pandas, and writes a formatted workbook with openpyxl. Five sheets:

1. **KPI Summary** — one-page headline numbers + a "how each metric is defined" block.
2. **Revenue & ROAS by Channel**
3. **Conversion Rate by Segment**
4. **Month-over-Month Trend** (incl. month-over-month revenue change)
5. **Top-Bottom 5 Performers** — channel × segment combinations ranked by ROAS.

ROAS cells are shaded **green** (≥ 1.0, profitable) or **red** (< 1.0, underwater). The metric definitions are baked into the workbook so a reviewer always sees how each number was computed:

- **ROAS** = revenue / spend
- **CTR** = clicks / impressions
- **Conversion rate** = conversions / clicks
- **CPA** = spend / conversions
- **Revenue per conversion** = revenue / conversions

The calculation functions are pure and unit-tested (`tests/`); the Excel formatting is separate and smoke-tested end-to-end.

## Findings

My read of the included synthetic campaign dataset (6 months, 1,571 rows; ~$334K spend, ~$636K revenue, **overall ROAS 1.91**):

- **Display is the only channel underwater.** Its ROAS is **0.81** — it consumes ~22% of the spend and a striking 58% of all impressions, yet returns just $60.4K on $74.5K, a roughly **$14K net loss**. It's the classic cheap-but-low-intent pattern: tons of cheap impressions, a thin 0.4% CTR, and the worst conversion rate of any channel.
- **Email is the most efficient by a wide margin — ROAS 3.60**, on a relatively small $48K budget, with the lowest CPA ($30). Paid Search (ROAS 2.27) is the workhorse: it carries the largest spend ($124K) and the most conversions while still returning well over 2x.
- **VIP is the standout segment.** It converts at **2.25%**, versus 1.23% Returning, 0.84% New, and 0.49% Dormant — roughly **1.8x Returning, 2.7x New, and 4.6x Dormant** (about 2.6x the average of the non-VIP segments). VIP also carries a much higher revenue-per-conversion (~$151), so its segment ROAS is ~5.0. The single best channel × segment combo is **Email × VIP at ROAS 9.5**; the worst combos are Display × Dormant (0.17) and Social × Dormant (0.20).
- **The trend is stable, not growing**, then ticks up at the end: monthly revenue bounces in a tight ~$97K–$112K band January through May before the strongest month, June, at $117K (**+13.4% MoM**, ROAS up to 2.18).

**What I'd recommend:** (1) **Cut or rework Display** — at sub-1.0 ROAS it's the only line actively losing money, so the fastest win is to pause it (or test a higher-intent placement/creative) and **redeploy that budget toward Email and the VIP segment**, which clear 3.6x and ~5x respectively. (2) **Lean harder into VIP and Returning** and pull spend off Dormant, where every channel combination is underwater. (3) Treat Paid Search as the dependable core to protect while experimenting elsewhere.

**What I'd check next** (this summary doesn't answer it): whether Display is truly worthless or just **mis-attributed** — view-through/assisted conversions could be landing in other channels, so I'd want a multi-touch attribution view before fully killing it. I'd also segment by *channel × segment over time* to see whether VIP's edge is steady or seasonal, and look at per-conversion economics net of CPA, not just ROAS.

*Caveat: this dataset is **synthetic** and seeded — the patterns are by design, so the point of the piece is the tool and the method, not these specific numbers.*

## How to run

```bash
# from this folder (04-excel-pivot-engine/)
pip install -r requirements.txt
python generate_demo_data.py             # writes data/demo.csv (already committed)
python pivot_engine.py data/demo.csv     # writes output/campaign_summary.xlsx
```

Runs in well under 30 seconds on the demo data. Point it at any CSV with the same columns:

```bash
python pivot_engine.py your_file.csv -o your_report.xlsx
```

### Running the tests (optional)

```bash
pip install -r requirements-dev.txt
pytest tests -v
```
